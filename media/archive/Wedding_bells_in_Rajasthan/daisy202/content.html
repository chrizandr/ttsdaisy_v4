When 23-year-old Pawan Kumar brought his bride from Madhya Pradesh to the nondescript Rajghat village in Rajasthan’s Dholpur district earlier this week, the villagers had much to rejoice about. The wedding bells had rung in here after 22 years as no parent was willing to marry their daughter off to anyone in the village. For over two decades, the shoddy state of Rajghat, situated 5 km away from Dholpur town, ruled out the possibility of its eligible bachelors getting any marriage proposals. The last marriage took place in the village in 1996. Situated on the banks of Chambal river, the small

Much has changed in the village thanks to Ashwani Parashar, a ﬁnal year MBBS student in the Sawai Man Singh Government Medical College in Jaipur and a native of Dholpur. He moved a Public Interest Litigation in the Rajasthan High Court on the conditions in Rajghat last year and wrote letters to the Prime Minister's Ofﬁce. He also launched a social media campaign with hashtag #SaveRajghat.
